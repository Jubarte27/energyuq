With the increasing demand for computational systems, resource usage, notably electricity usage, has become a pressing matter. That's why ensuring the resolution of these problems is as energy efficient as possible is the subject of extensive research.

Unfortunately, energy consumption is affected by many factors, some of which are difficult to predict or quantify. Fortunately, the science of uncertainty quantification (UQ) provides a whole set of statistical tools that allow modelling systems surrounded by uncertainty.

Using UQ, it is possible to better understand what affects energy efficiency in parallel applications the most, by analysing and quantifying the uncertainties in the system.

We aim to apply UQ to analyze a variety of parallel applications, measuring both energy consumption and execution times, while varying the machine's configuration (frequency, number of threads, etc.). Our goal is to create a model that relates these metrics, to better guide parameter choice.
